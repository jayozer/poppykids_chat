Q: When is a baby likely to start smiling at people?
A: Babies typically begin to smile socially around three months of age and start smiling spontaneously at people by four months.

Q: What are some effective ways to encourage a baby to laugh?
A: To encourage a baby to laugh, you can mimic their sounds, show excitement when they smile, repeat actions they enjoy, play interactive games like peek-a-boo, and provide age-appropriate toys such as rattles and picture books.

Q: What should I do if my baby isn't laughing by six months?
A: If your baby hasn't started laughing by six months old, it's recommended to consult with a doctor or nurse to rule out any developmental delays or hearing impairments.

Q: How does a baby's temperament affect their laughter?
A: Each baby's innate temperament can influence how frequently they laugh, as some may naturally smile more while others are less inclined to laugh.

Q: What milestones should I look forward to in my baby's first few months?
A: In the first few months, you can look forward to milestones such as your baby's first social smiles, spontaneous smiles, cooing, giggling, and eventually the first laugh and the eruption of their first teeth.

Q: At what age should parents start introducing a cup to their baby?
A: Parents can begin introducing a cup to their baby as early as 6 months of age.

Q: When is it recommended for babies to start weaning from the bottle?
A: UCSF Benioff Children's Hospital recommends that babies begin weaning from the bottle between 12 to 18 months of age.

Q: What are some signs that indicate a child is ready to transition from bottle to cup?
A: Signs that a child is ready for the transition include the ability to sit up on their own, following a set mealtime routine, and beginning to eat solid foods using a spoon.
Q: When does the complete set of primary teeth typically fully emerge?
A: The complete set of primary teeth, totaling 20, typically fully emerges by the time a child is two to three years old.
